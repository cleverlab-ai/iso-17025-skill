# ISO/IEC 17025:2017 — Audit Preparation Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare for accreditation or surveillance audits. For each item, verify that evidence exists and is current.

## 4. General Requirements

### 4.1 Impartiality
- [ ] Risk analysis for impartiality documented and current
- [ ] Personnel declarations of impartiality/conflicts of interest signed
- [ ] Evidence that identified risks have been eliminated or minimized
- [ ] Management commitment to impartiality documented

**Common nonconformities:** No formal risk identification process; risk analysis not updated after organizational changes; no declarations from new employees.

**Typical auditor questions:** "How do you identify risks to impartiality?" "Show me your most recent risk assessment." "What actions were taken for identified risks?"

### 4.2 Confidentiality
- [ ] Legally enforceable confidentiality commitments in place (contracts, NDAs)
- [ ] Personnel confidentiality agreements signed (including contractors, temporary staff)
- [ ] Procedure for handling confidential information
- [ ] Process for notifying clients when disclosure is legally required

**Common nonconformities:** No confidentiality agreements with external personnel; no procedure for handling client data requests from third parties.

## 5. Structural Requirements
- [ ] Legal entity status documented (registration, articles)
- [ ] Organizational chart current with reporting lines
- [ ] Scope of laboratory activities defined and documented
- [ ] Management responsibilities clearly defined
- [ ] Quality Manager (or equivalent) appointed with defined authority
- [ ] Procedures documented to necessary extent

**Common nonconformities:** Organizational chart outdated; scope document does not match actual activities; responsibilities not clearly defined for all positions.

## 6. Resource Requirements

### 6.2 Personnel
- [ ] Competence requirements defined for each position/function
- [ ] Personnel files with evidence of: qualifications, training, experience, competence assessments
- [ ] Training plan/records current
- [ ] Authorization records for specific activities (method development, result authorization, etc.)
- [ ] Supervision records for personnel in training
- [ ] Competence monitoring records (periodic assessment)

**Common nonconformities:** No competence criteria for specific functions; training records incomplete; no evidence of ongoing competence monitoring; unauthorized personnel signing reports.

**Typical auditor questions:** "Show me the competence requirements for [analyst name]." "How do you determine when someone is competent?" "Who is authorized to validate methods?"

### 6.3 Facilities and Environmental Conditions
- [ ] Facility requirements documented (temperature, humidity, cleanliness, lighting, noise, vibration)
- [ ] Environmental monitoring records (temperature/humidity logs, if required)
- [ ] Access control procedures and records
- [ ] Separation of incompatible activities documented
- [ ] Records of off-site work (if applicable)

**Common nonconformities:** Environmental monitoring gaps; no documented requirements for specific areas; inadequate separation of sample preparation and analysis areas.

### 6.4 Equipment
- [ ] Equipment list/inventory current
- [ ] Equipment records complete (identity, calibration, maintenance, location, condition)
- [ ] Calibration certificates available and current
- [ ] Calibration program/schedule maintained
- [ ] Equipment labels showing calibration status and due dates
- [ ] Procedures for handling, maintenance, transport, storage
- [ ] Records of equipment failures, malfunctions, modifications, repairs
- [ ] Out-of-service equipment properly identified and isolated
- [ ] Intermediate check procedures and records (where applicable)
- [ ] Software validated and version controlled

**Common nonconformities:** Overdue calibrations; incomplete equipment records; no intermediate checks where needed; no procedure for handling defective equipment; software not validated.

**Typical auditor questions:** "Show me calibration certificate for [instrument]." "What happens when calibration is overdue?" "How do you handle a suspect result from equipment malfunction?"

### 6.5 Metrological Traceability
- [ ] Traceability chain documented for all measurement results
- [ ] Calibrations performed by competent (accredited) laboratories
- [ ] Reference materials from competent (ISO 17034) producers with certificates
- [ ] Traceability to SI demonstrated (or appropriate reference where SI not possible)

**Common nonconformities:** Broken traceability chain; reference materials from non-accredited suppliers without justification; no traceability demonstration for specific measurements.

### 6.6 External Products and Services
- [ ] List of approved external providers (suppliers, subcontractors)
- [ ] Evaluation/selection criteria documented
- [ ] Monitoring and re-evaluation records
- [ ] Communication of requirements to providers documented

**Common nonconformities:** No formal evaluation of external providers; subcontracted work not identified in reports; no monitoring of supplier performance.

## 7. Process Requirements

### 7.1 Review of Requests, Tenders and Contracts
- [ ] Review procedure documented
- [ ] Records of reviews for recent contracts/orders
- [ ] Evidence of client communication regarding method changes/deviations
- [ ] Records of contract amendments and re-reviews

### 7.2 Methods
- [ ] Method index/register current
- [ ] Current versions of all methods available to personnel
- [ ] Method verification records (for standardized methods)
- [ ] Method validation records (for non-standardized/modified methods)
- [ ] Validation reports with characteristics (accuracy, precision, LOD, LOQ, uncertainty, etc.)
- [ ] Records of method deviations (documented, justified, approved)
- [ ] Method development records (if applicable)

**Common nonconformities:** Outdated method versions in use; no verification records for newly adopted methods; incomplete validation (missing parameters); validation acceptance criteria not defined.

**Typical auditor questions:** "Show me the validation report for [method]." "How do you verify a new method before first use?" "What parameters did you validate and why?"

### 7.3 Sampling
- [ ] Sampling plans and methods documented
- [ ] Sampling records complete (date, time, location, conditions, personnel, equipment, deviations)

### 7.4 Handling of Test/Calibration Items
- [ ] Sample receipt/identification procedure
- [ ] Unique identification system for all items
- [ ] Records of receipt conditions (deviations noted)
- [ ] Storage conditions monitored and recorded (where required)
- [ ] Sample retention and disposal procedures

### 7.5 Technical Records
- [ ] Records complete with all required information
- [ ] Records allow reproduction of activity under similar conditions
- [ ] Changes traceable (original data preserved, date of change, who changed, what changed)
- [ ] Electronic records backed up and secure

### 7.6 Measurement Uncertainty
- [ ] Uncertainty budgets for all reported measurements
- [ ] Uncertainty evaluation procedure documented
- [ ] All significant contributors identified and quantified
- [ ] Uncertainty reported correctly (units, significant figures, coverage factor)

**Common nonconformities:** No uncertainty budget for some methods; significant contributors omitted; uncertainty not updated when method/equipment changes; incorrect reporting format.

**Typical auditor questions:** "Show me the uncertainty budget for [measurement]." "How do you identify uncertainty sources?" "When do you re-evaluate uncertainty?"

### 7.7 Ensuring Validity of Results
- [ ] Quality control procedures documented (QC charts, reference materials, blanks, duplicates)
- [ ] QC data analyzed and reviewed regularly
- [ ] Control charts maintained with action limits
- [ ] Proficiency testing participation records (planned, results, corrective actions)
- [ ] Interlaboratory comparison results analyzed
- [ ] Actions taken for out-of-control situations documented

**Common nonconformities:** No systematic QC program; PT results not analyzed or acted upon; control charts not maintained; no action plan for unsatisfactory PT results.

### 7.8 Reporting
- [ ] Report format compliant with 7.8.2.1 (all required elements present)
- [ ] Reports reviewed and authorized before release
- [ ] Conformity statements include decision rule reference (7.8.6)
- [ ] Opinions/interpretations clearly identified and based on results (7.8.7)
- [ ] Amended reports properly identified with reference to original
- [ ] Subcontracted results clearly identified in reports

### 7.9 Complaints
- [ ] Complaint procedure documented and available
- [ ] Complaint register/log maintained
- [ ] Records of investigation, actions taken, outcomes
- [ ] Evidence of independence in complaint resolution

### 7.10 Nonconforming Work
- [ ] Procedure for nonconforming work documented
- [ ] Records of nonconformities, risk evaluations, actions taken
- [ ] Evidence of client notification when relevant
- [ ] Link to corrective actions when recurrence risk exists

### 7.11 Data and Information Management
- [ ] LIMS/software validation records
- [ ] Access controls implemented and documented
- [ ] Backup and recovery procedures tested
- [ ] Data integrity measures in place
- [ ] Audit trail functional (for electronic systems)
- [ ] System failure records and corrective actions

## 8. Management System Requirements (Option A)

### 8.2-8.4 Documentation and Records
- [ ] Quality policy and objectives defined, known to personnel
- [ ] Quality manual (or equivalent) current
- [ ] Document control procedure (approval, review, version control, distribution)
- [ ] Record control procedure (identification, storage, protection, retention, disposal)
- [ ] Master document list/register current
- [ ] Obsolete documents properly controlled

### 8.5 Risk and Opportunities
- [ ] Risk assessment documented and current
- [ ] Actions planned and implemented
- [ ] Effectiveness of actions evaluated

### 8.6 Improvement
- [ ] Improvement opportunities identified and implemented
- [ ] Customer feedback collected and analyzed

### 8.7 Corrective Actions
- [ ] Corrective action procedure documented
- [ ] Root cause analysis performed for nonconformities
- [ ] Corrective actions implemented and effectiveness verified
- [ ] Records complete (nature, cause, actions, results)

### 8.8 Internal Audits
- [ ] Audit program/schedule documented
- [ ] All clauses/activities covered in program cycle
- [ ] Audit reports complete with findings/observations
- [ ] Corrective actions from audits implemented and verified
- [ ] Auditor independence ensured (not auditing own work)

**Common nonconformities:** Incomplete audit coverage; overdue corrective actions from previous audits; no follow-up on findings; auditors not trained.

### 8.9 Management Reviews
- [ ] Management review conducted at planned intervals
- [ ] All required inputs addressed (see 8.9.2 a-o)
- [ ] Outputs include decisions and actions (effectiveness, improvement, resources, changes)
- [ ] Actions from previous reviews followed up
- [ ] Records of management review maintained

**Common nonconformities:** Not all required inputs covered; no evidence of action follow-up; reviews conducted but no documented outputs; reviews too superficial.

**Typical auditor questions:** "Show me the last management review." "What actions resulted?" "How were they followed up?"
