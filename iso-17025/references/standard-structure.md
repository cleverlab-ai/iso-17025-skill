# ISO/IEC 17025:2017 — Full Standard Reference

## 1. Scope
Specifies general requirements for competence, impartiality and consistent operation of laboratories. Applies to all organizations performing laboratory activities, regardless of personnel count. Used by laboratories, customers, regulatory authorities, accreditation bodies.

## 2. Normative References
- ISO/IEC Guide 99 (VIM) — International vocabulary of metrology
- ISO/IEC 17000 — Conformity assessment vocabulary

## 3. Terms and Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| 3.1 impartiality (bezstronnosc) | Maintaining objectivity. Absence of conflicts of interest. |
| 3.2 complaint (skarga) | Expression of dissatisfaction by any person/organization regarding laboratory activities or results |
| 3.3 interlaboratory comparison | Organization, performance and evaluation of measurements/tests on same/similar items by two or more laboratories |
| 3.4 intralaboratory comparison | Organization, performance and evaluation of measurements/tests on same/similar items within same laboratory |
| 3.5 proficiency testing | Evaluation of participant performance against pre-established criteria using interlaboratory comparisons |
| 3.6 laboratory | Entity performing one or more of: testing, calibration, sampling followed by testing/calibration |
| 3.7 decision rule | Rule describing how measurement uncertainty is accounted for when stating conformity with a specification |
| 3.8 verification | Provision of objective evidence that a given item fulfils specified requirements |
| 3.9 validation | Verification where specified requirements are adequate for intended use |

## 4. General Requirements

### 4.1 Impartiality
- **4.1.1** Laboratory activities shall be undertaken impartially, structured and managed to safeguard impartiality
- **4.1.2** Management shall be committed to impartiality
- **4.1.3** Laboratory shall be responsible for impartiality; shall not allow commercial, financial or other pressures to compromise impartiality
- **4.1.4** Laboratory shall identify risks to impartiality on ongoing basis (from activities, relationships, personnel relationships). Existence of relationships does not necessarily constitute risk
- **4.1.5** If risk identified, laboratory shall demonstrate how it eliminates or minimizes it

### 4.2 Confidentiality
- **4.2.1** Laboratory shall manage all information obtained/created during activities through legally enforceable commitments. Client informed in advance of information to be made public. All other information is confidential
- **4.2.2** When required by law to release confidential information, client/person shall be notified unless prohibited by law
- **4.2.3** Information about client from sources other than client shall be confidential between client and laboratory
- **4.2.4** Personnel (including contractors, committees, external bodies) shall maintain confidentiality

## 5. Structural Requirements
- **5.1** Laboratory shall be a legal entity or defined part thereof
- **5.2** Shall identify management with overall responsibility
- **5.3** Shall define and document scope of laboratory activities for which it conforms to this document
- **5.4** Laboratory activities shall meet requirements of this document, clients, regulatory authorities, and accreditation bodies. Includes activities at all permanent, temporary, mobile sites, and client sites
- **5.5** Laboratory shall: a) define organizational structure, b) define responsibilities/authorities of all personnel affecting results, c) document procedures to necessary extent
- **5.6** Shall have personnel with authority and resources for: a) QMS implementation/maintenance/improvement, b) identifying departures, c) initiating preventive actions, d) reporting to management, e) ensuring effectiveness
- **5.7** Management shall ensure: a) communication of QMS effectiveness and meeting requirements, b) integrity of QMS during changes

## 6. Resource Requirements

### 6.1 General
Laboratory shall have access to personnel, facilities, equipment, systems and support services needed.

### 6.2 Personnel
- **6.2.1** All personnel (internal and external) shall act impartially, be competent, work according to QMS
- **6.2.2** Shall document competence requirements for each function affecting results (education, qualifications, training, knowledge, skills, experience)
- **6.2.3** Shall ensure personnel are competent for activities they are responsible for, including assessing significance of deviations
- **6.2.4** Management shall communicate duties, responsibilities and authorities to personnel
- **6.2.5** Shall have procedures and records for: a) determining competence requirements, b) selection, c) training, d) supervision, e) authorization, f) monitoring competence
- **6.2.6** Shall authorize personnel for specific activities including: a) developing/modifying/verifying/validating methods, b) analyzing results including conformity statements and opinions/interpretations, c) reporting/reviewing/authorizing results

### 6.3 Facilities and Environmental Conditions
- **6.3.1** Facilities and environmental conditions shall be suitable and shall not adversely affect validity of results
- **6.3.2** Requirements for facilities shall be documented
- **6.3.3** Shall monitor, control and record environmental conditions as required
- **6.3.4** Measures for controlling facilities shall address: a) access control, b) prevention of contamination/interference, c) effective separation of incompatible activities
- **6.3.5** When working at sites outside permanent control, shall ensure facility requirements are met

### 6.4 Equipment
- **6.4.1** Shall have access to equipment required for correct performance (instruments, software, measurement standards, reference materials, reagents, consumables, auxiliary apparatus)
- **6.4.2** Equipment from outside permanent control — shall ensure requirements are met
- **6.4.3** Shall have procedure for handling, transport, storage, use, maintenance to ensure proper functioning and prevent contamination/deterioration
- **6.4.4** Shall verify equipment meets specified requirements before use
- **6.4.5** Equipment for measurements shall provide required accuracy and/or measurement uncertainty
- **6.4.6** Measuring equipment shall be calibrated when: accuracy/uncertainty affects reported results, and/or calibration is required to establish metrological traceability
- **6.4.7** Shall establish calibration program, reviewed and adjusted as needed
- **6.4.8** Equipment requiring calibration or having defined use period shall be labelled/identified for status
- **6.4.9** Equipment that is overloaded, mishandled, gives suspect results, shown defective or outside specifications shall be taken out of service — isolated, labelled, or marked as unfit until verified
- **6.4.10** When intermediate checks are needed to maintain confidence, shall be performed per procedure
- **6.4.11** When reference data corrections are needed, shall ensure corrections are updated and implemented
- **6.4.12** Shall take practical measures to prevent unintended adjustments that could invalidate results
- **6.4.13** Equipment records shall include: a) identity (software version), b) manufacturer/model/serial, c) verification of conformity, d) current location, e) calibration dates/results/criteria/next date, f) reference material documentation, g) maintenance plan/history, h) damage/malfunction/modification/repair details

### 6.5 Metrological Traceability
- **6.5.1** Shall establish and maintain metrological traceability through documented, unbroken chain of calibrations linking to SI
- **6.5.2** Shall ensure measurement results are traceable to SI through: a) calibration by competent laboratory, b) certified values of CRMs with traceability from competent producer, c) direct realization of SI units confirmed by comparison
- **6.5.3** When traceability to SI is not technically possible, shall demonstrate traceability to appropriate reference

### 6.6 Externally Provided Products and Services
- **6.6.1** Shall ensure only suitable external products/services are used, when: a) intended for own activities, b) provided directly to client, c) used to support activities
- **6.6.2** Shall have procedure and records for: a) defining/reviewing/approving requirements, b) defining criteria for evaluation/selection/monitoring/re-evaluation, c) ensuring compliance before use, d) taking actions from evaluations
- **6.6.3** Shall communicate requirements to external providers

## 7. Process Requirements

### 7.1 Review of Requests, Tenders and Contracts
- **7.1.1** Shall have procedure for review ensuring: a) requirements defined/documented/understood, b) capability and resources exist, c) appropriate methods selected, d) client informed of departures
- **7.1.2** Shall inform client if method recognized as unsuitable or outdated
- **7.1.3** When conformity statement required, specification/decision rule shall be clearly defined
- **7.1.4** Differences between tender and contract shall be resolved before work begins
- **7.1.5** Client shall be informed of all deviations from contract
- **7.1.6** When contract amended after start, review shall be repeated and changes communicated to all personnel
- **7.1.7** Shall cooperate with clients for monitoring activities related to laboratory's work
- **7.1.8** Records of reviews shall be retained, including significant changes and relevant client discussions

### 7.2 Selection, Verification and Validation of Methods

#### 7.2.1 Selection and Verification
- **7.2.1.1** Shall use appropriate methods including uncertainty evaluation and statistical techniques
- **7.2.1.2** Methods/procedures/documentation shall be current and accessible to personnel
- **7.2.1.3** Shall use latest valid version of method unless inappropriate
- **7.2.1.4** When client doesn't specify method, laboratory selects appropriate one and informs client
- **7.2.1.5** Shall verify it can properly perform chosen methods before use. Records retained
- **7.2.1.6** When method development is required, shall be planned activity with competent personnel, periodic reviews
- **7.2.1.7** Deviations from methods permitted only when documented, technically justified, authorized and accepted by client

#### 7.2.2 Validation of Methods
- **7.2.2.1** Shall validate non-standardized methods, lab-developed methods, standardized methods used outside scope, and modified standardized methods. Validation as extensive as necessary
- **7.2.2.2** Changes to validated methods shall be evaluated for impact; if significant, new validation required
- **7.2.2.3** Characteristics of validated methods shall be relevant to client needs and consistent with requirements
- **7.2.2.4** Shall retain validation records: procedure, specification, characteristics, results, fitness-for-purpose statement

**Validation characteristics may include:** measurement range, accuracy, measurement uncertainty, detection limit, quantification limit, selectivity, linearity, repeatability, reproducibility, robustness, sensitivity to matrix/interference, systematic measurement error.

### 7.3 Sampling
- **7.3.1** Shall have sampling plan and method when sampling. Method shall address factors to control to ensure validity
- **7.3.2** Sampling method shall describe: a) selection of samples/sites, b) sampling plan, c) preparation and treatment to obtain required item
- **7.3.3** Shall retain sampling records: a) reference to method, b) date/time, c) identification data, d) personnel identification, e) equipment used, f) environmental/transport conditions, g) diagrams/maps, h) deviations

### 7.4 Handling of Test/Calibration Items
- **7.4.1** Shall have procedure for transportation, receipt, handling, protection, storage, retention, return/disposal. Including all conditions necessary to protect integrity and interests of laboratory and client
- **7.4.2** Shall have unambiguous identification system maintained throughout responsibility period. System shall ensure items are not confused physically or in records
- **7.4.3** When receiving item, deviations from specified conditions shall be recorded. When doubt about suitability, shall consult client before proceeding
- **7.4.4** When items require storage/conditioning under specific environmental conditions, these shall be maintained, monitored and recorded

### 7.5 Technical Records
- **7.5.1** Shall ensure technical records contain sufficient information for repetition under conditions as close as possible to original. Shall include date, personnel identification, and verification of data/results. Original observations shall be recorded at time of performance
- **7.5.2** Shall ensure traceability of changes in technical records. Both original and amended data shall be retained with date of change, identification of changed aspects, and personnel responsible

### 7.6 Evaluation of Measurement Uncertainty
- **7.6.1** Shall identify uncertainty contributions. All significant contributions shall be considered, including from sampling, using appropriate methods of analysis
- **7.6.2** Calibration laboratories (including own equipment) shall evaluate measurement uncertainty for all calibrations
- **7.6.3** Testing laboratories shall evaluate measurement uncertainty. When method precludes rigorous evaluation, estimation based on theoretical knowledge or practical experience shall be used

### 7.7 Ensuring Validity of Results
- **7.7.1** Shall have procedure for monitoring validity of results. Monitoring shall be planned, reviewed and shall include (as appropriate): a) use of reference materials/QC materials, b) use of alternative calibrated equipment, c) functional checks, d) use of control/working standards with control charts, e) intermediate checks, f) replicate testing using same/different methods, g) retesting of retained items, h) correlation of results for different characteristics, i) review of results, j) intralaboratory comparisons, k) testing of blind samples
- **7.7.2** Shall monitor performance by comparison with other laboratories (proficiency testing, interlaboratory comparisons)
- **7.7.3** Data from monitoring shall be analyzed, used for control and improvement. When predetermined criteria not met, appropriate action shall be taken to prevent incorrect results

### 7.8 Reporting of Results

#### 7.8.1 General
- **7.8.1.1** Results shall be reviewed and authorized before release
- **7.8.1.2** Results shall be provided accurately, clearly, unambiguously, objectively, usually in a report. Shall contain all information agreed with client and necessary for interpretation. Reports are technical records
- **7.8.1.3** When agreed with client, results may be reported in simplified manner

#### 7.8.2 Common Requirements for Reports
- **7.8.2.1** Each report shall contain at minimum: a) title, b) laboratory name/address, c) place of performance (if different), d) unique identification, e) client name/contact, f) method identification, g) item description/identification, h) dates of receipt/testing/sampling, i) date(s) of performance, j) date of issue, k) reference to sampling plan/method, l) statement that results relate only to items tested, m) results with units, n) amendments/deviations/limitations, o) identification of authorizing person, p) clear identification of externally provided results
- **7.8.2.2** Laboratory responsible for all information in report except client-supplied information (which shall be clearly identified)

#### 7.8.3 Specific Requirements for Test Reports
Additional: a) specific test conditions, b) conformity statements (see 7.8.6), c) measurement uncertainty where applicable, d) opinions/interpretations (see 7.8.7), e) additional information required by methods/authorities/clients

#### 7.8.4 Specific Requirements for Calibration Certificates
Additional: a) measurement uncertainty, b) conditions of calibration, c) traceability statement (see Annex A), d) results before and after adjustment, e) conformity statements if relevant, f) opinions/interpretations if relevant

#### 7.8.5 Reporting Sampling
When responsible for sampling, additional: a) sampling date, b) unique identification of item sampled, c) sampling location (including diagrams/photos), d) reference to sampling plan/method, e) environmental conditions during sampling, f) information for uncertainty evaluation

#### 7.8.6 Reporting Statements of Conformity
- **7.8.6.1** When conformity statement is provided, shall document decision rule considering risk level (false acceptance, false rejection) and apply the decision rule
- **7.8.6.2** Conformity statement shall clearly identify: a) what results it applies to, b) which specifications/parts are met or not, c) decision rule applied

#### 7.8.7 Reporting Opinions and Interpretations
- **7.8.7.1** Only authorized personnel shall issue opinions/interpretations. Laboratory shall document basis
- **7.8.7.2** Opinions/interpretations shall be based on results and clearly identified as such
- **7.8.7.3** When communicated directly to client, records of conversation shall be retained

#### 7.8.8 Amendments to Reports
- **7.8.8.1** When amendment needed, change shall be clearly identified with reason
- **7.8.8.2** Amendments as additional document referencing original
- **7.8.8.3** When new report issued, shall be uniquely identified and reference original

### 7.9 Complaints
- **7.9.1** Shall have documented process for receiving, evaluating and making decisions on complaints
- **7.9.2** Description shall be available to interested parties. Shall confirm whether complaint relates to laboratory activities
- **7.9.3** Process shall include: a) description of receiving/validating/investigating/deciding, b) tracking and recording, c) ensuring appropriate actions taken
- **7.9.4-7** Shall gather and verify information, acknowledge receipt, provide progress reports, notify of outcome, decision by uninvolved person

### 7.10 Nonconforming Work
- **7.10.1** Shall have procedure when any aspect of work or results does not conform to own procedures, client requirements, or monitoring criteria. Procedure shall ensure: a) responsibilities defined, b) actions taken (including halt/repeat/withdrawal), c) risk evaluation performed, d) decisions on acceptance made, e) client notified when necessary, f) authority for resumption defined
- **7.10.2** Shall retain records of nonconforming work and actions (b through f)
- **7.10.3** When evaluation indicates nonconformity could recur or doubt exists about operations conformity, shall implement corrective actions

### 7.11 Control of Data and Information Management
- **7.11.1** Shall have access to data and information needed for laboratory activities
- **7.11.2** Laboratory information management system(s) shall be validated by the laboratory (including proper functioning of interfaces) before introduction. Shall be authorized, documented and validated before implementation. All changes (including configuration) shall be authorized, documented and validated
- **7.11.3** LIMS shall: a) be protected from unauthorized access, b) be safeguarded against tampering/loss, c) operate in environment meeting specifications (or for non-computerized systems ensure accuracy of records), d) be maintained for data/information integrity, e) include recording of system failures and appropriate corrective actions
- **7.11.4** When managed by external provider, laboratory shall ensure provider meets requirements
- **7.11.5** Instructions, manuals and reference data shall be readily available to personnel
- **7.11.6** Calculations and data transfers shall be checked appropriately and systematically

## 8. Management System Requirements

### 8.1 Options
- **8.1.1** Shall establish, document, implement and maintain a QMS. Option A or Option B
- **8.1.2** **Option A**: QMS including documentation (8.2), document control (8.3), records control (8.4), risk actions (8.5), improvement (8.6), corrective actions (8.7), internal audits (8.8), management reviews (8.9)
- **8.1.3** **Option B**: Laboratory with ISO 9001 system that supports and demonstrates clauses 4-7 fulfillment also meets 8.2-8.9

### 8.2 Management System Documentation (Option A)
- **8.2.1** Shall establish, document and maintain quality policies and objectives at all organizational levels
- **8.2.2** Policies shall address competence, impartiality and consistent operation
- **8.2.3** Management shall demonstrate commitment to development, implementation and continual improvement
- **8.2.4** All documentation, processes, systems, records shall be included in or linked to QMS
- **8.2.5** All personnel shall have access to applicable QMS documentation

### 8.3 Control of Management System Documents (Option A)
- **8.3.1** Shall control documents (internal and external) relating to fulfillment of this document
- **8.3.2** Shall ensure: a) approved before issue, b) periodically reviewed/updated, c) changes identified, d) relevant versions available, e) uniquely identified, f) prevention of unintended use of obsolete documents

### 8.4 Control of Records (Option A)
- **8.4.1** Shall establish and maintain legible records demonstrating fulfillment of requirements
- **8.4.2** Shall implement controls for identification, storage, protection, backup, archiving, retrieval, retention, disposal. Records retained per obligations and for access period. Access shall comply with confidentiality

### 8.5 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities (Option A)
- **8.5.1** Shall consider risks and opportunities to: a) ensure QMS achieves intended results, b) enhance opportunities, c) prevent/reduce undesired effects, d) achieve improvement
- **8.5.2** Shall plan: a) actions to address risks/opportunities, b) how to integrate and evaluate effectiveness
- **8.5.3** Actions shall be proportionate to potential impact on validity of results

### 8.6 Improvement (Option A)
- **8.6.1** Shall identify improvement opportunities and implement necessary actions
- **8.6.2** Shall seek customer feedback (positive and negative) and analyze/use for improvement

### 8.7 Corrective Actions (Option A)
- **8.7.1** When nonconformity occurs: a) react to control and correct, b) evaluate need for action to eliminate cause (through review, root cause analysis, determination if similar could occur), c) implement needed actions, d) review effectiveness, e) update risks/opportunities if necessary, f) make QMS changes if necessary
- **8.7.2** Corrective actions shall be appropriate to effects of nonconformities
- **8.7.3** Shall retain records of: a) nature, cause and subsequent actions, b) results of corrective actions

### 8.8 Internal Audits (Option A)
- **8.8.1** Shall conduct internal audits at planned intervals to determine if QMS: a) conforms to own requirements and this document, b) is effectively implemented and maintained
- **8.8.2** Shall: a) plan/establish/implement/maintain audit program, b) define audit criteria/scope, c) ensure results reported to management, d) implement corrections and corrective actions without undue delay, e) retain records
- ISO 19011 provides guidance on internal audits

### 8.9 Management Reviews (Option A)
- **8.9.1** Management shall review QMS at planned intervals to ensure continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness
- **8.9.2** Inputs shall include: a) changes in internal/external issues, b) fulfilment of objectives, c) suitability of policies/procedures, d) status of previous review actions, e) internal audit results, f) corrective actions, g) external assessments, h) changes in scope/activities, i) customer/personnel feedback, j) complaints, k) improvement effectiveness, l) adequacy of resources, m) risk identification results, n) validity assurance results, o) other relevant factors
- **8.9.3** Outputs shall include decisions and actions relating to: a) QMS effectiveness and processes, b) improvement of laboratory activities, c) provision of required resources, d) any needed changes

## Annex A — Metrological Traceability (Informative)
- Traceability established through: documented unbroken chain of calibrations linked to SI, with uncertainty evaluated at each step
- Systematic measurement error (bias) of calibrated equipment is accounted for when ensuring traceability
- Calibration certificates with only conformity statements (without measurement results and uncertainties) may be used for traceability when specification limits serve as uncertainty source
- Ways to demonstrate traceability: a) calibrations by NMIs/DIs under CIPM MRA, b) calibrations by accredited laboratories under ILAC/regional arrangements

## Annex B — Management System Options (Informative)
- Option A: Laboratory establishes own QMS per clauses 8.2-8.9
- Option B: Laboratory uses ISO 9001 QMS that already covers clauses 4-7 requirements
- Both options equally valid for accreditation; choice depends on laboratory's existing systems
